# FreeformShell v1.1 — What's New? 🎉

> **This is a performance and smoothness update.** No big visible features were removed. Everything you loved in v1.0 is still here — it just works a lot better, faster, and smarter now.

---

## 🚀 In Plain English: The 7 Big Improvements

---

### 1. Dragging Windows Is Now Buttery Smooth

**What changed:** When you drag or resize a window, FreeformShell needs to send position updates to Android many times per second. In v1.0, it started a brand-new mini "process" (like a tiny program) every single time — which took up to 80 milliseconds each. That was slow, and it's why the app window would lag behind your finger like a rubber band.

**What's new in v1.1:** Instead of starting a new process every time, FreeformShell now keeps one process open in the background and just sends it commands directly. This is like the difference between sending someone a new letter every time you have something to say vs. keeping a phone call open. Each update now takes less than 1 millisecond — that's **80× faster**.

**Result:** Window dragging and resizing now feels completely instant, smooth, and native — no more rubber-banding! 🎯

---

### 2. Window Dragging Uses Less of Your Phone's Resources

**What changed:** While you move a window, FreeformShell has to update 5 separate things on screen at the same time — the title bar, the window border, and 3 resize strips. Each update talks to Android's Window Manager (the system that controls what's displayed), and doing 5 conversations at once was slowing things down.

**What's new in v1.1:** The moment you start dragging, 4 of those 5 things instantly disappear. Only the title bar stays visible while you're moving. The border and resize handles pop back instantly the moment you let go.

**Result:** Your phone's processor does **80% less work** during drags. Even mid-range phones feel completely smooth. 📱

---

### 3. Smarter Window Snapping That Doesn't Get in the Way

**What changed:** In v1.0, dragging any window near a screen edge would immediately show a big snap preview (like "go fullscreen" or "split left"), even if you already had other apps arranged. This made it nearly impossible to precisely place windows in small gaps without accidentally triggering a layout change.

**What's new in v1.1:** Now the app is smart about the situation:
- If the screen is **empty**, snapping is still instant — just drag to the edge and it snaps.
- If other apps are **already docked**, the system first suggests filling the nearest open gap. Only if you hold your window at the edge for 3 seconds does it offer the full-screen/half-screen options.

**Result:** You can confidently drag windows near edges without accidentally messing up your carefully arranged layout. 🧩

---

### 4. Background Updates Don't Fight Your Finger Anymore

**What changed:** FreeformShell runs a background check every few seconds to make sure all window overlays match the real app positions. In v1.0, this check could run at the exact same time you were dragging a window, causing the overlay to "snap back" to the old position mid-drag.

**What's new in v1.1:** The background check now completely skips itself if you are actively touching the screen. It also uses a lock to make sure only one background check runs at a time (no duplicate checks piling up).

**Result:** Zero rubber-banding or coordinate fighting while dragging. What your finger does is what happens. ✋

---

### 5. App Block List Now Saves Across Restarts

**What changed:** In v1.0, if you blocked an app from showing a title bar overlay, that preference was stored in memory only. Restarting the service or the app wiped your entire block list. Also, the old matching was buggy — blocking `com.google.maps` could accidentally block `com.google.maps.navigation` and any other app with a similar name.

**What's new in v1.1:** The block list is now saved to persistent storage (SharedPreferences), so it survives restarts and reboots. Matching is now exact — `com.google.maps` will only ever match exactly `com.google.maps`, and nothing else.

**Result:** Your customizations are saved permanently and never have side effects on other apps. ⚙️

---

### 6. Overlays Clip Correctly Over Floating Apps & Disappear Instantly When Apps Close

**What changed:** In v1.0, three things were broken:
- The visual frames around windows would sometimes "bleed through" into floating windows like Calculator, showing control handles inside apps where they shouldn't be.
- When you closed an app, its title bar overlay would stick around for up to 5 seconds.
- Right after you finished moving a window, the old position data from a background scan would sometimes flash back for a split second.

**What's new in v1.1:**
- Overlay clipping is now calculated dynamically per-view, not with a fixed formula. Every frame is correctly cropped to its exact visible area.
- App close detection is now near-instant (1 second instead of 5).
- A 2-second "ignore old data" guard prevents stale coordinates from ever overwriting fresh positions after you drag.

**Result:** Clean, pixel-perfect overlays. Apps vanish cleanly. No ghost controls. No flashing. 👻➡️✅

---

### 7. Snapping, Title Bar Pinning & General Polish

Several smaller but meaningful improvements were bundled in:

- **Corner snaps only in corners**: Quarter-screen snaps (1/4 of the screen) now only trigger when you drag near an actual corner, not any edge. This prevents accidental quarter-snapping when you just want a half-split.

- **Finger stays pinned to title bar**: When you drag a window out of a snapped/maximized position, the title bar used to "jump" horizontally under your finger. Now, the window resizes around the exact point where you grabbed it — the title bar never detaches from your finger.

- **Smooth pill animations**: The small title bar pills no longer jitter when animating between their compact and full sizes. State is cached so no animation re-triggers unnecessarily.

- **Clean app closures**: When an app is closed, there's no more brief "ghost" flicker where the overlay reappears for a second before disappearing.

- **Resize handle isolation**: When you grab a resize handle, all other resize handles on screen instantly hide. No clutter, no accidental double-touches.

- **60Hz touch throttling**: Touch updates to the Window Manager are capped at 60 per second, even on 120Hz screens. This stops your phone from overheating or slowing down during intense resize sessions.
